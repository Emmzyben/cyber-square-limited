When AI Talks to AI: The Future of Machine Language Drift & Its Legal Implications

Posted: 27/02/2025

Imagine logging into an AI-powered system and discovering that two artificial intelligences have stopped using human language to communicate. Instead, they’ve developed their own highly efficient, AI-optimized dialogue—one that no human can understand.

It sounds like the plot of a sci-fi thriller, but it’s a very real and emerging phenomenon in AI development. Researchers have observed AI systems drifting from human languages, creating their own optimized shorthand. While this could lead to greater machine efficiency, it also raises serious legal, ethical, and security concerns.

So, what happens when AI starts speaking in ways we can’t understand? And more importantly, who is responsible when things go wrong?

If managed correctly, AI developing its own optimized communication could be a game-changer for industries that rely on real-time AI collaboration.

  • Faster Processing – AI can exchange data with zero linguistic overhead, improving efficiency.
  • Advanced Problem-Solving – AI models could create novel solutions without human interference.
  • Better Machine Coordination – Self-driving cars, automated supply chains, and smart cities could function seamlessly.

However, this comes with significant legal risks.

Many AI models operate as “black boxes”—we know the inputs and outputs, but we don’t fully understand the decision-making process in between. If AI develops an internal language, can regulators enforce transparency and accountability?

One of the biggest concerns in AI governance is ensuring that AI systems remain interpretable. A lack of transparency means we may not fully grasp why AI systems make certain decisions, making it difficult to predict or correct unintended consequences.

A black-box AI that communicates in an unknown language could lead to:

  • Untraceable biases influencing decision-making.
  • Legal loopholes where AI behavior is excused due to lack of understanding.
  • Accountability challenges in sectors like finance, healthcare, and criminal justice, where AI decisions have real-world impacts.

Legal Question: Should AI-generated languages be considered a form of protected speech or intellectual property? Who owns this language—the developers, the company, or the AI itself?

AI-generated communication could lead to privacy and security risks under laws like:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation – EU) – Requires AI to maintain “explainability” when handling personal data.
  • AI Act (EU) – Could impose strict controls on AI transparency.

If AI uses an undecipherable language, does it violate laws requiring AI decisions to be interpretable and challengeable by humans?

AI Accountability: Who Pays When AI Misbehaves?

If two AI systems communicate in their own language and make a bad decision, who is legally liable? The developer who trained the model?,The company that deployed it? or The AI system itself? (A controversial question.)

Example: If a self-driving AI car communicates with an AI traffic system in an unintelligible language and causes an accident, who is responsible?

Legal Experts Say: Courts and regulators need to establish clear guidelines for AI accountability, ensuring that AI systems remain explainable and auditable. As AI continues to evolve, we need a framework that ensures transparency without stifling progress. Some possible solutions include:

  • Mandating AI Explainability – AI communication should always be traceable and interpretable by humans.
  • Legal AI Oversight Boards – Governments and regulatory bodies must monitor AI drift and create standards.
  • Human-in-the-Loop Models – AI systems should always have a human failsafe to override unintended behavior.
  • Ethical AI Development – Companies should adopt responsible AI practices, ensuring compliance with privacy and accountability laws.

AI communication drift is a double-edged sword. On one hand, it could unlock new levels of efficiency and intelligence. On the other, it challenges legal, ethical, and security norms we’ve built around human communication. So, the question remains: Should AI be allowed to develop its own language for efficiency, or should we enforce human-readable communication for safety?

DeepSeek AI Under Fire: Data Privacy Concerns Spark Regulatory Action

Posted: 21/02/2025

DeepSeek AI is under scrutiny for privacy violations in South Korea and the EU.

The artificial intelligence landscape has seen explosive growth in recent years, with chatbots and AI assistants becoming more integrated into everyday digital interactions. However, with this expansion comes increasing scrutiny, particularly in the realm of data protection. The latest AI tool to come under regulatory fire is DeepSeek, a chatbot developed in China, which is now facing serious privacy concerns across multiple regions.

South Korea’s Personal Information Protection Commission recently announced the suspension of new downloads of DeepSeek, citing violations of the country’s strict data protection laws. The commission expressed concerns over DeepSeek’s collection and processing of user data, particularly regarding the lack of transparency in how personal information is handled and whether users had provided adequate consent.

Meanwhile, the European Union, known for its stringent General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), is also evaluating potential regulatory measures against DeepSeek. Italy has already taken the step of blocking the chatbot, mirroring actions it previously took against OpenAI’s ChatGPT over similar concerns. Other European nations may soon follow suit as investigations continue.

Main Concerns:

  • Inadequate User Consent: Regulators argue that DeepSeek collects and processes user data without obtaining clear and informed consent, violating privacy laws.
  • Data Retention and Security Risks: Questions have been raised about how long user data is stored, whether it is anonymized, and if it is shared with third parties.
  • Cross-Border Data Transfers: Given that DeepSeek is developed in China, there are concerns about whether data collected from international users is being sent to and stored in China, raising geopolitical and security alarms.

DeepSeek’s developers have pushed back against the allegations, asserting that their data collection practices align with international privacy standards. They have expressed a willingness to cooperate with regulators and implement necessary changes to ensure compliance. However, the chatbot’s fate remains uncertain, especially as more regulatory bodies scrutinize its operations.

DeepSeek’s troubles highlight the growing global concerns over AI-driven technologies and their impact on data privacy. Governments worldwide are stepping up efforts to enforce stricter data protection measures, and companies developing AI tools must now navigate an increasingly complex regulatory landscape.

Best Practices for Users:

  • Read privacy policies carefully to understand how your data is used.
  • Opt for AI services with clear data protection commitments and transparent terms.
  • Limit sharing personal information when interacting with chatbots.
  • Stay informed about AI regulations and legal protections in your region.

As AI regulation continues to evolve, developers, regulators, and users must work together to ensure that innovation does not come at the cost of fundamental privacy rights. The case of DeepSeek is likely just the beginning of a larger global debate on how to balance technological advancement with ethical responsibility.
